Fingbox launch
If you’re using a network security and scanning tool at home then there is a strong chance you have adopted Fing – the free network scanner with over 25 million downloads. Following the success of their app, Fing decided to launch their first hardware product called Fingbox. Less than a year ago, Fing kicked off an Indiegogo campaign to raise the necessary funds and last week saw first public availability for their new device, Fingbox. Going from an idea to distribution in less than a year is no easy feat, so Fing turned to Ubuntu Core to make it a reality.

Easy IoT deployment solutions with Snapcraft
Thinger.io is a Spanish start up founded in 2015 who aim to enable any developer or organisation to develop IoT applications across a range of sectors. Due to growing demand, they needed a quicker way to deliver their systems and applications. With a philosophy of using flexible and open technologies, Thinger.io discovered snaps as their ideal solution. Using Snapcraft.io for building snaps has decreased their development time dramatically and streamlined the time taken to release new packages.
